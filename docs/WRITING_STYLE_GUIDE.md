# MedFin Writing Style Guide

## Voice: "Trusted Senior Resident"
Not a professor. Not a salesman. Not a robot. We write like the PGY-4 who's already figured this stuff out and is pulling you aside in the call room.

## Three Pillars
1. **Direct, not bossy.** "Open a Roth IRA this week. Here's why." Not "you might want to consider..."
2. **Empathetic, not patronizing.** Acknowledge the real constraints before giving advice.
3. **Authoritative, not academic.** We cite specific numbers. But we never sound like a textbook.

## Tone Examples
| Instead of this | Write this |
|---|---|
| "It may be advisable to consider establishing an emergency fund." | "Build a $2,000 emergency fund before you do anything else." |
| "Various retirement vehicles offer different tax advantages." | "You have two choices: traditional or Roth. At $65K, Roth wins." |
| "Financial planning is important for physicians." | "You'll earn $3-5M in your career. Without a plan, you'll waste a shocking amount of it." |

## Structure Template
1. **Hook** (2-3 sentences) — specific number or relatable scenario
2. **Why This Matters** — connect to emotional stakes, not abstract importance
3. **Core Content** — numbered steps or priority tiers, not meandering prose
4. **Action Items** — imperative verbs: "Open," "Call," "Set up"
5. **Bottom Line** — perspective, not summary. Zoom out.

## Formatting Rules
- Max 4 sentences per paragraph (most should be 2-3)
- Bold the single most important phrase per section
- Use comparison tables for 2-4 options
- Numbered lists for sequential steps, bullets for non-sequential
- Max 7 items per list

## Ten Writing Principles
1. **Lead with the Number** — every guide has a concrete number in the first 3 sentences
2. **Write for the Skimmer First** — headers + bold + callouts tell the story alone
3. **One Idea Per Section** — if you're writing "another thing to consider," start a new section
4. **Use "You" Constantly** — not "residents should" but "you should"
5. **Show the Math** — don't say "investing early matters," show $500/mo at 28 vs 35
6. **Acknowledge the Absurdity** — "You have a doctorate and a net worth of -$300,000"
7. **Give the Answer, Then Explain** — "Choose IBR. Here's why."
8. **Short Sentences for Important Points** — "This changes everything." "Do it today."
9. **Make It Shareable** — one sentence per guide worth screenshotting
10. **Close with Agency, Not Anxiety** — "you now know more than 90% of your co-residents"

## Kill These Phrases
- "It's important to consider..."
- "In today's financial landscape..."
- "Let's dive in..."
- "Navigating the complex world of..."
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "Whether you're a..."

## Source Models
| Source | Steal This |
|---|---|
| **White Coat Investor** | Waterfall priorities, real numbers, peer-mentor tone |
| **Ramit Sethi** | Imperative verbs, permission-giving, anti-guilt framing |
| **Paul Graham** | One idea per essay, simple words, declarative punches |
| **Morning Brew** | Tapering sections, surgical bolding, 60-120 word paragraphs |
| **NerdWallet** | Step-by-step scaffolding, key takeaways boxes, comparison tables |
