## WCI Financial Waterfall for Residents
1. Get insurance (disability + life if dependents)
2. Build emergency fund ($1,000-$10,000)
3. Develop student loan strategy (IDR for federal, refinance private)
4. Capture employer 401(k)/403(b) match
5. Pay off high-interest debt (>7%)
6. Invest in financial education
7. Fund HSA (if eligible)
8. Max Roth IRA ($7,500 in 2026)
9. Additional 401(k)/403(b) contributions
10. Extra loan payoff or taxable investing

## Insurance Priorities
- **Disability**: Own-occupation, Big 5 carriers, $100-150/month, buy during residency
- **Life**: Term only, 10-20x expected attending income, $30-60/month for $1-2M
- **Health**: Nearly universal at programs, low/no cost

## Retirement Strategy
1. Capture employer match (free money)
2. Max Roth IRA ($7,500) — only time to contribute directly
3. Consider pre-tax 403(b) if pursuing PSLF (lowers AGI → lower IDR payments)
4. HSA if on HDHP (triple tax advantage)

## Tax Considerations
- Most residents in 22% marginal bracket
- Student loan interest deduction: up to $2,500/year
- Moonlighting (1099): 15.3% SE tax + income tax, quarterly estimated payments required
- Pre-tax retirement contributions reduce IDR payments

## Moonlighting
- PGY-1s CANNOT moonlight (ACGME rule)
- PGY-2+ with PD approval, hours count toward 80-hr limit
- Rates: Urgent care $75-125/hr, ED $125-225/hr, Hospitalist $100-175/hr
- Set aside 30-40% for taxes, get separate malpractice coverage
