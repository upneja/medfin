# Community Wisdom: The Medical School-to-Residency Transition

## Key Themes Summary

| Theme | Core Community Message |
|---|---|
| **Student Loans** | Get on an IDR plan immediately. Submit PSLF forms annually. Never choose forbearance. |
| **Budgeting** | You can live on a resident salary. Use the 50-30-20 rule. Automate everything. |
| **Insurance** | Disability insurance is non-negotiable. Buy it in intern year. Own-occupation only. |
| **Retirement** | Max your Roth IRA now -- you'll never be in this low a tax bracket again. |
| **Impostor Syndrome** | Everyone feels it. It's universal, expected, and temporary. Talk about it. |
| **Asking for Help** | The #1 clinical survival tip across every community. Never too proud to call. |
| **Self-Care** | Shower when you get home. Eat real food. Exercise. Protect your sleep. |
| **Preparation** | Don't study before residency. Rest. Recharge. Handle logistics. |
| **Long-term Finance** | "Live like a resident" for 2-5 years as an attending. #1 wealth-building rule. |

## Top 5 Loan Mistakes (Student Loan Planner)
1. Failing to consolidate into Direct Loans (only Direct qualify for PSLF)
2. Not verifying employer PSLF eligibility
3. Skipping annual PSLF Employment Certification
4. Choosing the wrong repayment plan
5. Choosing forbearance instead of IDR

## Budgeting Framework
- Average PGY-1: ~$65-68K → ~$4,000/month net
- 50-30-20 rule: essentials / flexible / savings+debt
- Housing: $800-$1,500/month (roommates recommended in expensive cities)
- Emergency fund target: 6 months expenses
- Top tools: YNAB, Empower, PocketGuard, Monarch Money

## Retirement Priority Order
1. Capture employer 401(k)/403(b) match
2. Max Roth IRA ($7,500) — only time in career to contribute directly
3. HSA if on HDHP

## Insurance Non-Negotiables
- 25% of physicians will experience a disability lasting 90+ days
- Own-occupation definition required
- Buy during intern year (youngest = cheapest)
- GSI policies skip medical underwriting

## Post-Match Checklist
**Immediately:** Read match email, complete onboarding, get BLS/ACLS/PALS
**Within Weeks:** File taxes, apply for IDR, submit PSLF form, consolidate loans, buy disability insurance, start apartment hunting
**Before Starting:** Eliminate CC debt, set up emergency fund, build budget, arrive 1 week early
**DON'T:** Study. Rest and recharge instead.

## Emotional Landscape
- 33-44% of residents experience impostor phenomenon
- Higher in women (49.4% vs 23.7% men) and IMGs
- Community consensus: This is normal, expected, temporary
- Key coping: Connect with co-interns, ask for help, use program mental health services, journal
